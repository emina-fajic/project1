Sirnica (Bosnian Cheese Pie)

If you are Bosnian, then you know what "sirnica" is. It is a pie filled with mixture of cheese, eggs and sour cream, and this is the definition of comfort food. Feeling depressive? Make some sirnica, taste it, and let your worries be washed away with how happy and satisfied you will feel after making an tasting this awesome pie.

Time to prepare:2 hours

Ingredients:

    For the Dough:
  • 1 kilogram of plain flour
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 300 mililiters of water
  • a bit of sunflower oil
    • For the Filling:
  • 4 eggs
  • 500 grams of fresh cheese
  • 3 tbsps of sour cream
  • 5 tsps of salt
  • 2 tbsps of sunflower oil
    • For the sauce:
  • 100 grams of sour cream
  • 50 grams of butter
  • 50 mililiters of oil

Preparing:

Start by making your dough. Mix upp around 700 grams of flour with 300 mililiters of water, and knead (best with your hand) in order to obtain a firm dough. If the dough is too runny and not firm enough, add more flour from what is left, but add just a bit by but of it, because this dough is delicate. When you have obtained a smooth dough that doesn't runs and stick everywhere around the bowl, pour the rest of the flour that is left from 1 kg into the bowl, shape your dough into a ball/circle, and tuck it in the flour. Let the flour coat the dough from each side. Leave it to set for at least an hour.

While the dough is setting, we can prepare the filling. Put your fresh cheese in a bigger bowl, and add sour cream and eggs. Mix it up until you get homogeneous mixture. Then, add the salt and the sunflower oil, and mix it up again, and that would pretty much be it.

When the dough is nicely set, place it on your worktop (you need a larger area, I use my dining room table). Roll your dough up into a larger circle, and then put around 1 tablespoon of sunflower oil and spread it across your dough. Then, start pulling the dough from the center to the edges of your table, from each side, so, in the end, you obtain a large table-like spreaded dough. Pull the dough gently, because you don't want it to break, but even if it does, it's not a problem, it just bothers me so I don't like when it happens.

When the dough is spreaded nicely, divide it into 3 even parts with a knife. Put 1/3 of the filling on one side of 1/3 of the dough, and roll it up so you obtain a long rolled stick. Repeat the process for each part.

Take a baking train and spread one tablespoon of sunflower oil across it, and then place your dough inside the tray, in round-motion, so at the end it looks like a snake. Where one part ends, begin with another part and repeat the process until you run out of dough. When your pie is all rounded up, coat it with 1 tablespoon of sunflower oil on top, and put it to a preheated oven, on 250 degrees Celcius. Bake it 10 minutes like that, and then decrease the temperature on 180 degrees and bake it 10-20 more minutes, until it gets all nice and goldenbrown.

Mix up all the ingredients for the sauce, heat them up and let them come to boil. When the pie is baked, coat it with hot sauce. If you hear how the pie sizzles when you put the sauce, it's a good sign. Cover it up with a larger baking tray and leave it like that for 5 minutes, to soften up a bit.

After that, there is nothing left but to eat it. Watch out, don't burn your tongue!

Enjoy!
